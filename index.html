ABOUT NAVRATRI

Navratri is a vibrant Hindu festival that spans nine nights, dedicated to worshiping Goddess Durga and her nine different forms. Celebrated with great enthusiasm across India, it symbolizes the victory of good over evil. Each day is associated with a different form of the goddess, embodying strength, wisdom, and protection.

CELEBRATIONS

Navratri celebrations are marked by vibrant and devotional activities throughout the nine days. In many parts of India, devotees worship different forms of the goddess Durga, offering prayers, performing aarti (rituals), and maintaining fasts. Traditional dance forms like Garba and Dandiya are popular in states like Gujarat, where people gather in large groups, dressed in colorful attire, to dance and celebrate the goddess. Temples are beautifully decorated, and cultural programs are held. In some regions, stages are set for plays (Ramlila) depicting the life of Lord Rama, leading to the grand celebration of Dussehra on the tenth day, symbolizing the victory of good over evil.
